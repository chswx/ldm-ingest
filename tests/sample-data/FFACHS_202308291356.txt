* WHAT...Flash flooding caused by excessive rainfall continues to be

* WHERE...Portions of southeast Georgia, including the following 
  areas, Coastal Bryan, Coastal Liberty, Coastal McIntosh, Jenkins 
  and Screven. Portions of southeast South Carolina, including the 
  following areas, Allendale, Dorchester, Hampton, Inland Berkeley, 
  Inland Colleton and Tidal Berkeley.

* WHEN...From Wednesday morning through late Wednesday night.

* IMPACTS...Excessive runoff may result in flooding of rivers, 
  creeks, streams, and other low-lying and flood-prone locations.

* ADDITIONAL DETAILS...
  - Tropical Cyclone Idalia is forecast to track along the
    southeast Georgia and southeast South Carolina coastline
    after making landfall in the Big Bend region of Florida early
    Wednesday morning. Rainfall is forecast to begin over
    southeastern Georgia as early as Wednesday morning,
    overspreading the region through Wednesday afternoon and into
    Wednesday night, with rainfall totals of 4 to 8 inches
    forecast. The heaviest rainfall is expected to occur
    Wednesday evening, coinciding with the high tide,
    exacerbating the flash flooding threat.
  - http://www.weather.gov/safety/flood
